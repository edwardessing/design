    I’d like to make it clear that I was living in London. Because there is a difference between travelling and living. The principle benefit of living is that you won't be as haunted by ‘tomorrow’. Travelling through Continental Europe I felt oppressed by the unforgiving pace. There was an exhausting obligation to get the most out of each destination. Living in a city you don’t feel committed to see this or that, you could always do it some other day (…never happens). It’s a very unique and powerful experience and study abroad so happens to be one of the few opportunities, as a student, that offers it.
    I lived in UAL’s Hackney accommodation - which housed over four hundred students. You can research the room and technical specs yourself, I won’t bore you. Your experience, your joy, your whatever…will chiefly depend on the people you meet (not the size of your bed). I met some really great people. Bless you all.
    If you’re reading this and considering a semester abroad; I apologise for possibly setting some pretty unrealistic expectations. To get the most out your time I would encourage getting to know the other international students. They were in the same position as me - they elected to do the exchange program to UAL. Although there will naturally be differences between what influenced their decision and yours, the outcome is mutual. And by that measure, it is likely that you will meet someone like-minded. Crazy.
