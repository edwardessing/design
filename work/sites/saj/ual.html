    UAL - which stands for the University of the Arts London - is the largest art school in Europe. Accordingly, it provides for some 19,000 students from over 100 countries. I did the ‘free-elective program’. The free-elective program involved one of three core subjects (fashion, graphic design, or studio art) and a range of electives dispersed over 14 weeks.
    My core was graphic design with electives in conceptual editing, cultural studies, typography and, as my personal therapy, fashion illustration. I have never felt so productive and creative over a single school semester. Credit for this goes to: the worldly tutors; the nicely-sized classrooms and enlivening atmosphere; the novel course structure, namely the faster project turnover; connection to the work environment; and the absence of those reliable, familiar options (i.e. your favourite printing place).
